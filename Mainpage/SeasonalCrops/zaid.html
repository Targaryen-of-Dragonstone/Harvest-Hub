Zaid Cropping Season

Zaid cropping season falls between the Rabi and Kharif seasons and is characterized by warm, dry weather. Crops in the Zaid season are typically sown in March and harvested in June.

Zaid crops include cucumbers, pumpkins, tomatoes, and bitter gourds, among others. These crops thrive in the summer heat and are an essential source of income for farmers.

In addition to providing income, Zaid crops also contribute to the nutritional diversity of diets and fulfill the demand for seasonal fruits and vegetables.
