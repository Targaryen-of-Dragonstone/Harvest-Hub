Rabi Cropping Season

The Rabi cropping season, also known as the winter season, is an important agricultural period in India. It typically starts in October or mid-November after the monsoon season ends.

Rabi crops are sown during this season and harvested in April or May, depending on the crop type. These crops are well-suited to the cooler weather conditions of winter.

Major Rabi crops grown in India include wheat, mustard, barley, peas, chickpeas, cumin, fennel, coriander, and gram. These crops play a vital role in ensuring food security and providing income to farmers.
